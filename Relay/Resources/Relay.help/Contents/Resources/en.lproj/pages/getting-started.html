Get started with Relay

Relay is a native macOS client for Matrix. This page covers the basics: what Matrix is, how to sign in, and how to find your way around the app.

What is Matrix?

Matrix is an open, federated chat network. Your account lives on a homeserver — a server that stores your messages and account data. Because Matrix is federated, you can communicate with users on any other homeserver, much like email. Your full identity looks like @user:server.org.

Sign in to Relay

Relay supports two ways to sign in:

  • Password: Enter your full Matrix ID (for example, @alice:matrix.org) and your password.
  • OAuth / SSO: For homeservers that support it, Relay opens a browser window where you complete authentication through your homeserver’s login flow.

If your homeserver uses a non-standard URL, expand the Advanced section on the sign-in screen and enter the homeserver URL manually.

Choose a homeserver

When creating a new account, Relay presents a curated list of public homeservers to choose from. You can also specify a custom server URL if you run your own homeserver or use one that isn’t listed.

Find your way around

Relay uses a three-column layout:

  • Space rail (left edge) — Switch between your spaces. Includes Home to show all rooms and a button to create a new space.
  • Sidebar (middle) — Your room list. Use the search field to find rooms, filter by All, Rooms, or DMs, change the sort order, and access pinned rooms.
  • Detail area (right) — The conversation timeline for the selected room, the room inspector, or a space detail view.

Quick Switch

Press ⌘K to open the Quick Switch overlay. Start typing a room name to jump to it instantly without scrolling through the sidebar.

Tip: You can reopen this help book at any time from Help › Relay Help in the menu bar.
