Make and join calls

About MatrixRTC calls

Relay uses MatrixRTC with LiveKit for audio and video calls. Calls are end-to-end encrypted in encrypted rooms. Your homeserver must have a LiveKit SFU configured to support calls.

Start a call

  1. Click the Call button in the toolbar of any room.
  2. A confirmation dialog appears.
  3. The call opens in a dedicated window.

Join an ongoing call

When someone else starts a call in a room, the toolbar button changes to Join Call. Click it to join.

Call controls

The call window has a translucent floating control bar at the bottom:

  • Microphone toggle: mute or unmute your microphone.
  • Camera toggle: enable or disable your camera.
  • End call: the red phone button disconnects you from the call.

Multi-participant calls

With multiple participants, video feeds arrange in a responsive tile grid. Each participant's tile shows their video, name, and microphone status. A glow effect indicates who is currently speaking.

Connection phases

When connecting, Relay shows step indicators as it contacts the call server and sets up the media connection. This typically takes a few seconds.

Important: If Relay shows a permissions error, you may need to grant microphone and camera access in System Settings > Privacy & Security.

Note: Calls require your homeserver to have MatrixRTC configured. If you see a credentials error, ask your homeserver administrator about MatrixRTC support.
